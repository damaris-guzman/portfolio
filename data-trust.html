The process began with 3D printing miniature versions of my original piece, Unlock-it. I used these prints as molds and, with the support of the SJSU foundry, translated them into metal casts. In the installation, the lockets are displayed alongside metal works by my incredibly skilled colleagues. What began as objects I was making for myself unexpectedly found their way into the exhibition.

In April, I was working on this okra locket for the Data Trust exhibition by Stephanie Dinkins in a class led by Esteban Garcia Bravo. This pendant is modeled after the cross-section of an okra pod; a nod to seed-bearing vessels and personal memory.

A scalloped brass shell frames a glass inset that reveals the pod's interior geometry: ten walnut-hued "seeds" (micro 3-D prints) encircle a faceted emerald core. Hinge it open and the seeds sit flush against the viewer, transforming a common vegetable slice into a miniature reliquary that stores growth, nourishment, and hidden value close to the heart.
